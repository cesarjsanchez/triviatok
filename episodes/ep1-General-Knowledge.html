The original Trivia Tok round mixes geography, art, science, literature, chemistry, Nobel prizes, and classic general knowledge facts.

Questions and answers

General Knowledge Trivia

Question 1: What is the longest river in Europe?

Answer: Volga River

Question 2: Which painter is known for his series of paintings featuring water lilies?

Answer: Claude Monet

Question 3: In which decade was the first modern Olympic Games held?

Answer: 1890s (first modern Olympic Games held in 1896)

Question 4: What is the smallest planet in our solar system?

Answer: Mercury

Question 5: Which famous scientist developed the three laws of motion?

Answer: Sir Isaac Newton

Question 6: What is the term for a word that is spelled the same way forwards and backwards?

Answer: Palindrome

Question 7: In which U.S. state is the Grand Canyon located?

Answer: Arizona

Question 8: Which philosopher wrote the book 'The Republic'?

Answer: Plato

Question 9: What are the three primary colors of light?

Answer: Red, green, and blue

Question 10: Who won the Nobel Peace Prize in 1964?

Answer: Martin Luther King Jr.

Question 11: Which country is known as the Land of the Rising Sun?

Answer: Japan

Question 12: What type of energy is stored in chemical bonds?

Answer: Chemical energy

Question 13: Which Shakespearean play features the characters Rosencrantz and Guildenstern?

Answer: Hamlet

Question 14: What is the chemical formula for table salt?

Answer: NaCl (sodium chloride)

Question 15: Who was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize?

Answer: Marie Curie

Question 16: How many keys are there on a standard computer keyboard?

Answer: 101-104 keys (standard QWERTY keyboard)

Question 17: What is the tallest mountain in Africa?

Answer: Mount Kilimanjaro

Question 18: Who is the author of the dystopian novel 'Brave New World'?

Answer: Aldous Huxley

Question 19: Which element is represented by the chemical symbol 'Au'?

Answer: Gold

Question 20: Which famous composer became deaf in his later years?

Answer: Ludwig van Beethoven
